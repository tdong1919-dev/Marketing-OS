## The Value of One Discovery Call

Before calculating the impact of three additional calls per week, we need to establish what a discovery call is actually worth.

A discovery call is not a cold interaction.

It is a prospect who has already shown interest.

They have:

- Engaged with your content
- Asked a question
- Entered a conversation
- Agreed to discuss their needs

They are much closer to a purchasing decision than someone seeing your brand for the first time.

Discovery call close rates vary depending on industry, offer, sales process, and lead quality.

For coaching, consulting, and agency businesses, average close rates commonly range from 20%–30%, with stronger operators achieving higher conversion rates with qualified inbound prospects.

For this model, we will use a conservative:

**25% discovery call close rate**

Meaning:

1 out of every 4 qualified discovery calls becomes a customer.

### Annual Impact

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many discovery calls should a service business book per week?**

There is no universal number because it depends on pricing, capacity, and sales process. However, even a few additional qualified discovery calls per week can create significant revenue growth when multiplied over a year.

**What is a good discovery call close rate?**

A typical qualified discovery call close rate for service businesses often falls around 20–30%, although businesses with strong positioning, strong offers, and qualified inbound prospects may achieve higher conversion rates.

**How do Instagram comments become customers?**

Instagram comments become customers when businesses recognize buying signals, start conversations, understand the prospect's needs, and create a clear path toward a purchase decision.

A comment alone does not create revenue.

**Does posting more content increase sales?**

Posting more content can increase opportunities, but content alone does not guarantee revenue. Businesses need systems that capture interest and move prospects through the buying journey.

**Why do businesses lose potential customers online?**

Common reasons include:

- Slow response times
- Missed comments
- Inconsistent follow-up
- No clear conversion process
- No system for identifying interested prospects
